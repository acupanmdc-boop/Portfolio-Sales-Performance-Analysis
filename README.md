# Portfolio-Sales-Performance-Analysis
Sales Performance Analysis
**Executive Overview**
The business delivered strong profitability with a 41% margin but slightly missed its sales target. Performance is heavily driven by Bikes, highlighting both a strength and a risk due to limited diversification
**Problem Statement**
The main problem was to determine whether the company is meeting its sales targets and whether profitability is sustainable across different product categories.
Additionally, we wanted to identify if the business is overly dependent on a single product.

   **KPI Overview**

  However, despite this, the business achieved a strong profit of 6.81 million, with a high profit margin of 41.37%, indicating efficient cost management**
   **Problem**
  The main problem was to determine whether the company is meeting its sales targets and whether profitability is sustainable across different product  categories.Additionally, we wanted to identify if the business is overly dependent on a single product.
  **Action**
   To address the main problem, I created a Power BI dashboard analyzing sales, budget, and profit by product category.
   I used KPI cards for summary metrics, a Sales vs Budget chart for performance comparison, and a Profit by Product chart to understand contribution
 **Sales vs Budget**
  Looking at the product-level performance, Bikes generated 15.85 million in sales, slightly exceeding its budget.
Accessories and Clothing also met or slightly exceeded their targets.
However, overall sales still fell slightly below total budget, indicating minor underperformance.
 **Profit Analysis**
  In terms of profitability, Bikes contributed the majority with 6.27 million in profit.
Accessories contributed 0.42 million, while Clothing contributed only 0.13 million.
This clearly shows that Bikes dominate the business in terms of profit
**Key Insights**
From the analysis, there are four key insights:
First, the company slightly missed its overall budget.
Second, the business is highly dependent on Bikes.
Third, profit margins are strong, which is a positive sign.
And lastly, other product categories have very low contribution, indicating lack of diversification.
**Recommendations**
  Based on these findings, I recommend the following:
First, diversify revenue streams by investing more in Accessories and Clothing.
Second, increase sales efforts to close the gap with the budget.
Third, implement bundling strategies, such as selling Bikes with Accessories.
Fourth, focus on high-margin opportunities.
And finally, reduce dependency on Bikes to minimize business risk.
